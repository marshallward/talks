Barotropic solver

Barotropic solver

Basic equation: $$\begin{align} \eta_t &= -\nabla \cdot \mathbf{U}_\text{bt} + \mathbf{F}_\text{src} - \mathbf{F}_\text{smooth} \\ \left( \mathbf{U}_\text{bt} \right)_t &= - f \hat{z} \times \mathbf{U}_\text{bt} - g \nabla \left(\eta - \eta_\text{tide} \right) + \mathbf{F}_\text{src} + \mathbf{F}_\text{fric} \end{align}$$

  • Predictor-corrector (Euler) timesteps
  • 80 barotropic steps per ocean (baroclinic) step
  • Halos updated every 10 BT timesteps
  • 42 halo updates per ocean step

Free surface smoothing

Free surface smoothing

Biharmonic diffusion of free surface: $$\begin{align} \eta_t &= \ldots + \nabla^4 \left(\eta + \eta_\text{min} \right) \\ \left( h \phi \right)_t &= \ldots + \nabla^4 \left[ \left( \eta + \eta_\text{min} \right) \phi \right] \end{align}$$

  • One call per (baroclinic) timestep
  • 1 collective $\left( \eta_\text{min} \right)$
  • 14 halo updates $\left( 5 + 3 N_\text{tracers} \right)$

However...

  • Laplacian smoothing is less work, and more stable!
  • C-grids do not need smoothing!

  3. Poor IO performance
